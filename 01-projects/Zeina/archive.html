  • Massport Comment Letter on the Environmental Notification Form (ENF)
-This comment letter lays out the impacts of the Winthrop Square Development on the operations of Logan Airport, including the issue of the building height intruding upon the airspace of Logan and how that impacts Runway 27 resulting in a shifting of traffic to Runway 33L.



Bus Stop Data:

  • Bus Stop Excel Sheet
-This document was generated to locate the bus stops found in East Boston and describe their current conditions. It states whether the stop is sheltered or not and what the surroundings are (greenspace, roadways, residential or schools). The purpose of compiling this information was to understand what the possible needs are and how to address what specific bus stops may need.

Historical Documents:

  • “Airports: Some Elements of Design and Future Development” by John Walter Wood, 1940.
-The chapter pertaining to the “Boston Airport” is scanned in this document. The excerpt is of interest as it was written in the late 1930s, prior to the establishment of plans to expand the airport with the eventual taking of Wood Island Park in the 1960s. At the time of the writing of this book Boston Airport’s growth is described as having been “irregular and haphazard” with “no adequate provision [being] made in the layout of the airport for future expansion.” The chapter also includes some interesting photos of the airport from this era and a map of the airport.
  • Michael Philip Manheim’s Photos
-For the Documerica Project (1971-1977), the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) hired freelance photographers to capture images relating to environmental problems, EPA activities, and everyday life in the 1970s. Michael Philip Manheim was one of nearly one hundred photographers hired for DOCUMERICA. His assignment was to document the noise pollution crises in the East Boston neighborhood around Neptune Road. Manheim captured powerful images of the deteriorating community, illustrating its uncomfortable proximity to one of the nation’s busiest airports and the plight of residents living under the landing path of jets on the approach to Logan’s busy runways.
  • East Boston Community News Archive (Northeastern University)
-Northeastern University has scanned and makes freely available the entire 1970 – 1989 run of the locally produced weekly newspaper that covered the happenings of East Boston, including much of the events concerning the expansion plans of the airport and the community’s resistance to it.
